# Positioning Plan

## Core Positioning

Position around AI agents and automation for business workflows, while keeping
trading software as a separate technical specialty.

Do not position as an AI trading-return provider. Trading clients often judge
the work by returns, not by whether the software was built correctly. That
creates unrealistic expectations and makes technically successful projects look
like failures when the strategy does not make money.

Use this positioning:

> I build AI agents and automation systems for business workflows such as
> research, reporting, customer operations, ecommerce, CRM, and internal tools.
> I also build trading software, broker API integrations, market-data tools,
> backtesting systems, and execution dashboards, but I do not sell AI as a
> guaranteed trading-return system.

## Lane 1: Research And Reporting Agents

Build agents that collect, structure, summarize, monitor, and report on
information.

Good fit:

- market and competitor research
- web monitoring and alerts
- document summarization
- cited research reports
- recurring business reports
- data extraction into structured outputs
- due-diligence assistants

Buyer outcome:

- save research time
- produce repeatable reports
- monitor changes without manual checking
- turn unstructured information into usable business output

Proposal angle:

> I can build an agent that gathers the sources, extracts the important
> information, structures it, and produces a repeatable report with human review
> points where needed.

## Lane 2: CRM And Sales Ops Agents

Build agents that support sales, follow-up, lead qualification, CRM hygiene, and
pipeline operations.

Good fit:

- lead qualification
- CRM updates and enrichment
- follow-up draft generation
- meeting prep
- sales-call summaries
- pipeline summaries
- quote or proposal preparation
- email and calendar workflow support

Buyer outcome:

- faster follow-up
- cleaner CRM data
- less manual admin work
- better sales visibility
- more consistent sales process

Proposal angle:

> I can build an AI-assisted workflow that reads the lead/customer context,
> updates the CRM, drafts the next action, and keeps a human approval step before
> anything sensitive is sent.

## Lane 3: Ecommerce And Business Ops Agents

Build agents that help ecommerce and operations teams manage repetitive work,
catalogs, customer issues, reviews, and internal processes.

Good fit:

- product catalog cleanup
- product description generation with review
- customer support assistants
- review and feedback analysis
- competitor/product monitoring
- inventory or pricing alerts
- order/admin workflow automation
- Google Sheets, Airtable, Notion, Slack, email, and CRM integrations

Buyer outcome:

- reduce repetitive admin work
- improve product and customer data quality
- surface operational issues faster
- support sales and conversion decisions
- connect existing tools without rebuilding the whole business system

Proposal angle:

> I can build an agent that connects to your existing tools, processes the
> repetitive work, produces structured outputs, and leaves approval checkpoints
> for anything customer-facing or business-critical.

## Separate Specialty: Trading Software

Keep trading software as a technical credibility lane, not as an AI-agent
returns promise.

Good fit:

- broker API integrations
- exchange API integrations
- market-data pipelines
- execution dashboards
- backtesting systems
- TradingView, MetaTrader, NinjaTrader, Interactive Brokers, Alpaca, Binance
- alerts, scanners, portfolio/risk tools

Boundary language:

> I can build the trading software, data pipeline, execution workflow, or
> backtesting environment. I do not promise that an AI model or strategy will
> generate trading returns.

## Jobs To Avoid

- AI trading bot promising profit
- "guaranteed returns" trading systems
- vague AI alpha-generation projects
- generic chatbot projects with no clear workflow or data source
- low-budget website or Shopify setup work
- pure visual design work with no automation or engineering component

## Operating Rule

Lead with AI agents and automation. Use trading software as proof of technical
depth, but keep it separate from AI trading-return promises.
